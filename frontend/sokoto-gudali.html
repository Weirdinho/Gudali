SOKOTO GUDALI(BOKOLO)

Overview

According to the Nigerian National Livestock Research Survey (NNLRS), Gudali cattle represent 32% of the national herd. There are two distinct types of Gudali cattle in Nigeria: the Sokoto Gudali (Bokolo) and the Adamawa Gudali. The Sokoto Gudali is stereotypically found in the North-West of Nigeria, but it is now distributed widely throughout the country (Katie & Alistair, 1986; Williamson & Payne, 1990; Payne & Wilson, 1999).

Physical Characteristics

The Sokoto Gudali is characterized by its uniform cream, light grey, or dun coat. It has a highly developed dewlap and skin folds, with almost absent horns. The hair is short, and the skin is thick and pigmented. The ears are pendulous.

Diary Attributes

The Sokoto Gudali are useful milkers, with a milk yield at the National Animal Production Research Institute (NAPRI), Shika, higher than that of the White Fulani (Payne & Wilson, 1999; Alphonsus et al., 2012). They have a calving interval of 360-450 days. The udders in females are well developed with good teats, making them an indigenous dairy breed.

Weight & Milk Yield

At maturity, the female Sokoto Gudali weighs an average of about 330 kg, while the male weighs about 450 kg. The female produces an average of 1,500 kg of milk per lactation (Payne & Wilson, 1999).
