KURI

Overview

The Kuri is a large-bodied, humpless long-horn cattle breed with an unknown historical origin.

Physical Characteristics

Kuri cattle are noted for their distinctive, inflated, spongy horns, which are unique among cattle breeds. They have a mean height of 1.5 meters and can weigh up to 550 kg, making them one of the largest breeds of African cattle. Kuri cattle exhibit extremely variable colors and have the ability to thrive in semi-aquatic conditions.

Geographical Distribution

The nucleus of the Kuri cattle population is within the region of the former Lake Chad and along its eastern shores. In Nigeria, Kuri cattle are found not only in Lake Chad but also on its shores and along the Yobe valley, extending as far west as Gashagar.

Usage and Crossbreeding

There is a restricted export of Kuri cattle as traction animals to the region northeast of Kano. The breeds along the Komadugu Yobe are often crossed with the Zebu and are generally referred to as Jetkoram in the literature.
