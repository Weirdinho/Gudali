WADARA

Overview

Wadara cattle are another notable Nigerian breed, representing approximately 6.6% of the national herd.

Physical Characteristics

Wadara cattle are medium-sized and lightly built. They usually have dark-red, black, pied, or brown coats. These cattle are short-horned and have a small erect hump.

Distribution & Significance

Wadara cattle are indigenous to the Borno region. They are referred to as 'our' cattle by the Koyam and related pastoralists. In the literature, they are often called 'Shuwa' after the Shuwa Arabs who also herd them.

Related Breeds

A related breed with a white coat, known as Ambala, is often traded into Nigeria from Chad (Blench, 1993; Meghen et al., 1999).
