AZAWAK

Overview

The Azawak is another breed found in Nigeria, native to the Azawak valley in the North-East of Nigeria and distributed along its North-Western border.

Physical Characteristics

Azawak cattle are lightly built with medium-length horns. While Azawak cattle in Niger Republic are commonly described as red, those that enter Nigeria are usually a light fawn color, though they can also be white, brown, pied, or black.

Population

The Nigerian National Livestock Research Survey (NNLRS) 1990 estimated that Azawak cattle represent just 0.7% of the national herd. A small population of Azawak cattle exists in Nigeria throughout the year, but the majority is seasonally transhumant.

Distribution

Azawak cattle are generally found on the border North and West of Sokoto, with some in the North-West of Borgu and dotted along the frontier from Sokoto to Katsina.
