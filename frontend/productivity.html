PRODUCTIVITY

Critical Nutrents

Protein, energy, and minerals are the most critical nutrients affecting milk and beef production in semi-arid areas.

Nutrents Limitations

Some reports suggest that energy and minerals are not limiting nutrients for grazing cattle. Instead, deficiencies in protein are attributed to losses in cattle productivity.
However, Poppi and McLennan (1995) and Devendra and Sevilla (2002) reported that during the early to mid-wet season, rangelands are inadequate sources of energy and minerals. This inadequacy results in low cattle growth rates and stands as a major constraint to increasing body weight gains, consequently affecting beef production in semi-arid areas.
Generally, rangeland energy and mineral supplies in the late wet and dry seasons are sufficient to meet the production requirements of cattle on pastoral systems in semi-arid areas.

Availability and Production Efficiency

Cattle production efficiency on communal rangelands in semi-arid areas is often determined by nutrient availability, which is mainly influenced by temperature and seasonal distribution of rainfall.

Spatial grazing and Optimization

Pastoralists optimize spatial resource use through extensive grazing, allowing the soil to rejuvenate and preventing permanent land damage.

Season Variation in Feeding Resources

The impact of seasonal variation in feed resources on the condition and performance of cattle grazing in the Guinea Savannah Zone of Nigeria has not been fully established. Such information is critical for developing appropriate feeding and disease prevention strategies. For example:
(1) The abrupt diet change that cattle experience from one season to another leaves them prone to digestive upset.
(2) Although the switch from a forage diet to a finishing diet high in grain gives beef its desired marble characteristic, it also upsets the animals' digestive system, adversely impacting their growth.
(3)Marked seasonal quantity and quality of feed resources affect cattle performance and carcass quality.

Traditional Cattle Sector in Nigeria

The traditional cattle sector in Nigeria is characterized by low productivity due to the seasonality of quantitative and qualitative feed shortages. This is a major constraint to improved production and productivity of smallholder enterprises.
